Which company will the U.S. government take a stake in next? Here's what traders think
Following disclosed U.S. government equity stakes in quantum computing firms, prediction market platform Kalshi shows elevated probability on IonQ, Micron, and defense-tech unicorn Anduril as next targets. IonQ shares are in focus as the highest-probability pure-play quantum name, while Micron adds a semiconductor memory angle to the government-backed investment thesis. Options traders should watch IonQ short-dated calls for momentum plays and consider Micron upside spreads given any stake announcement would likely trigger a sharp gap higher.
Despite murky legal landscape, companies are undeterred in their prediction market investments
Multiple companies reaffirmed expansion plans for prediction market businesses during recent earnings calls despite ongoing regulatory uncertainty at the CFTC and state levels. This signals management confidence that the regulatory outcome will be favorable, keeping sector sentiment constructive even as headline risk remains elevated. Traders can express a bullish view via Kalshi-adjacent public equities or watch for volatility compression in names like CME Group, which benefits from any formalization of prediction market regulation under its existing exchange framework.
Surge in 'risk-free' treasury yields sends bond investors in search of better opportunities
Treasury yields have surged materially, undermining the traditional risk-free narrative and forcing fixed-income allocators to reassess duration exposure across portfolios. Relative value is emerging in intermediate maturities, BBB-rated corporate credit, and select high-yield names where spread compensation now more than offsets incremental default risk. Rates traders should consider steepener positions on the 5s30s curve, while credit desks can look at BBB-to-HY crossover names via CDX IG versus HY spread differentials for tactical carry opportunities.
U.S. pushes its AI in China and Asia after Trump-Xi meeting
A senior U.S. official for APEC and economic policy confirmed that embedding American AI technology across Asian markets is a top diplomatic and commercial priority following the Trump-Xi summit. This opens a significant revenue expansion narrative for large-cap U.S. AI infrastructure and software names seeking re-entry or deeper penetration into previously restricted Asian markets. Traders should focus on hyperscaler and AI chip names like Nvidia, Microsoft, and Palantir for near-term upside, with call spreads offering defined-risk exposure ahead of any formal bilateral technology framework announcements.
Stocks making the biggest moves premarket: IMAX, Merck, Estee Lauder & more
IMAX, Merck, and Estee Lauder are among the most active names in premarket trading on Friday, driven by a mix of earnings revisions, guidance updates, and sector-specific catalysts. Estee Lauder continues to face pressure from sluggish Asia-Pacific demand and a challenged prestige beauty cycle, while Merck's move likely reflects pipeline or pricing newsflow. Options traders should check implied volatility versus realized moves in each name to identify whether the premarket gap is already priced into same-day expiry contracts or if further directional continuation trades remain viable.
