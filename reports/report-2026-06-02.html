FS KKR Sells $900 Million Bonds in Rare Junk-Rated BDC Deal
FS KKR Capital Corp, a publicly traded business development company jointly managed by FS Investments and KKR & Co., sold $900 million in high-yield (junk-rated) bonds on Monday in what sources described as an unusually large and rare offering for a BDC in the public bond market. The deal is notable because BDCs — which lend to middle-market companies — rarely access the high-yield bond market at this scale, making the transaction a significant data point for the private credit and leveraged finance landscape. The bond sale reflects continued investor appetite for yield in the current rate environment, even as it highlights the growing role of private credit vehicles in mainstream capital markets.

Polymarket closes its first block trade as prediction markets push for Wall Street adoption

The move is part of a broader push by prediction market platforms to position themselves as legitimate financial instruments for institutional use, competing for a share of the risk-transfer and price-discovery functions traditionally dominated by Wall Street derivatives markets. Polymarket's expansion into block trading reflects the growing mainstream interest in prediction markets as a financial product, following increased visibility during recent election cycles and major geopolitical events.
