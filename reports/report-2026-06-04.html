As Strait of Hormuz stays shut, companies seek new routes for trade
Shipping companies and global trade operators are actively seeking alternative maritime and overland routes as the closure of the Strait of Hormuz continues to disrupt the flow of goods through one of the world's most critical chokepoints. The Strait of Hormuz is a vital passage for global energy and cargo shipments, and its prolonged shutdown has forced logistics firms to reroute vessels around longer and more costly paths, adding to supply chain expenses and delivery timelines. The disruption is contributing to elevated energy market uncertainty, with Brent crude at $94.79 and broader commodity markets reflecting the geopolitical risk premium embedded in current prices.
