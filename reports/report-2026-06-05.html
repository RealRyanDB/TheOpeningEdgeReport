Stocks and Bonds Fall as Jobs Fuel Fed-Hike Bets: Markets Wrap
Wall Street's historic weekly winning streak is on track to end Friday after a stronger-than-expected jobs report reignited speculation that the Federal Reserve's next interest-rate move could be a hike rather than a cut. The solid labor market data pushed Treasury yields higher across the curve, with the 10-year rising to 4.49% and the 2-year climbing to 4.08%, while both equities and bonds sold off simultaneously — an unusual dynamic that removes the traditional portfolio hedge. The dual selloff in stocks and bonds reflects a market recalibrating its Fed policy expectations, with futures now implying a Fed Funds rate closer to 3.65% and rate-cut bets being scaled back heading into the June 17-18 FOMC meeting.
With Jobs Market Stable, Fed Is Focusing on Inflation Over Rate Cuts
Federal Reserve officials have shifted their primary focus toward resurgent inflation rather than supporting the labor market with rate cuts, according to reporting that reflects the Fed's evolving policy calculus in mid-2026. With the unemployment rate at 4.3% and jobless claims recently falling to their lowest level since mid-May, policymakers no longer see an urgent need to ease financial conditions to protect employment. The Fed's pivot in focus comes as CPI data showed a monthly increase of +0.85% in April 2026, and officials are now watching the June 12 CPI release and June 25 Core PCE print closely before making any policy adjustments at or after their June meeting.
Dow Jones Futures Fall, Snowflake Surges On Earnings; Fed Inflation Data Due - Investor's Business Daily
