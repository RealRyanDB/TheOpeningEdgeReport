The U.S. adds 172,000 jobs. Many are in restaurants, bars and hotels
The U.S. economy added 172,000 jobs in May, marking the third consecutive month of job growth, with the unemployment rate holding steady at 4.3% according to the latest government data. The bulk of the gains were concentrated in the leisure and hospitality sector — restaurants, bars, and hotels — rather than higher-wage industries, and wage growth softened in a way that likely failed to keep pace with rising consumer prices. The report was seen as a mixed signal for the Federal Reserve, as continued job creation supports a resilient labor market narrative, but the softening wage growth and sector composition of gains may temper concerns about a wage-price spiral ahead of the June 12 CPI release.
