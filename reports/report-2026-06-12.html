What is a Stock Buyback?
After walking away from a bid for Warner Bros. Discovery, Netflix announced a $25 billion plan to repurchase its own shares, bringing its total buyback capacity to approximately $32 billion. A stock buyback occurs when a company uses its cash to purchase its own shares from the open market, reducing the total number of shares outstanding. This practice is common among S&P 500 companies, meaning investors who hold broad index funds are frequently exposed to companies actively conducting repurchase programs.

The S&P 500 already made a big call on SpaceX stock and index fund investors need to know it
The S&P 500 index declined to include SpaceX following its IPO, which is being described as the largest in history.
