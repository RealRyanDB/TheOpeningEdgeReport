The S&P 500 already made a big call on SpaceX stock and index fund investors need to know it
The S&P 500 index excluded SpaceX from its benchmark following the company's IPO, meaning index funds tracking the S&P 500 will not automatically hold shares in the newly public company. S&P Dow Jones Indices applies profitability and float requirements that SpaceX did not meet for inclusion. Investors with retirement savings in S&P 500 index funds will therefore have no direct exposure to SpaceX through those holdings.
How the historic SpaceX IPO is turning everyday workers into overnight millionaires
SpaceX completed what is being described as the largest IPO in history, with the public listing converting employee stock options into substantial wealth for current and former workers. Many employees had accumulated options over years of service at the private aerospace company, which was valued at hundreds of billions of dollars before going public. Workers have begun disclosing plans for their newfound liquidity as shares began trading on public markets.
Best Biotech Stocks Right Now

Justice Dept. approves Paramount's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery
The U.S. Department of Justice granted antitrust approval for Paramount's proposed $111 billion acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, clearing a major regulatory hurdle for the deal. The merger would combine Paramount, the parent company of CBS, with Warner Bros. Discovery, which owns HBO, CNN, and a broad portfolio of film and television assets. The approval paves the way for the two media giants to complete their combination and form one of the largest entertainment companies in the world.
