Warsh's Hawkish Turn Has Scrambled the Math on Rates
Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh has signaled a firm commitment to combating inflation, prompting futures markets to price in at least one interest rate increase in 2026. Warsh, who took over leadership of the Fed, has adopted a notably hawkish stance compared to recent policy expectations. The shift has forced a broad repricing of rate expectations across fixed income and equity markets.
Dow Jones Futures Fall, Snowflake Surges On Earnings; Fed Inflation Data Due - Investor's Business Daily
Dow Jones futures declined in early trading while Snowflake posted strong earnings results that sent its shares sharply higher. The moves come ahead of a closely watched Federal Reserve inflation data release scheduled for later in the day. Equity markets have been volatile as investors reassess the interest rate outlook under the new Fed leadership.
Chairman Warsh abstains from giving rate forecast as several members signal a hike in 2026
Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh declined to submit a personal rate forecast in the latest Summary of Economic Projections, breaking from the standard practice of most Fed chairs. The median projection among Fed members placed the federal funds rate at 3.8% by the end of 2026, a quarter percentage point above the current target range. Several Fed officials explicitly signaled support for at least one rate hike before year-end.
