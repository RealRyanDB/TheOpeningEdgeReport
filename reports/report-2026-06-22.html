RBC Says Stocks Can Weather Fed Hikes, But Not Too Many
RBC Capital Markets head of US equity strategy research Lori Calvasina stated that strong corporate earnings are currently supporting equity markets despite Federal Reserve interest rate hike concerns. The Fed has been raising rates in an effort to combat persistent inflation, creating uncertainty across financial markets. Calvasina noted that while earnings provide a buffer, the market's resilience has clear limits depending on the number of additional hikes that materialize.
Stock market today: S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow futures muted with peace talks, AI demand in focus
Futures for the S&P 500, Nasdaq, and Dow Jones Industrial Average are trading in a subdued range as of Monday morning. Ongoing geopolitical peace negotiations and continued demand signals in the artificial intelligence sector are the two primary themes drawing investor attention. The muted futures activity reflects a market in a holding pattern as participants assess how both developments may influence near-term economic conditions.
What to look for before buying your first ETF
Exchange-traded funds provide investors with broad exposure to various market sectors, often at a lower cost than purchasing individual stocks. The structure of ETFs allows for diversification within a single instrument, making them a common starting point for new investors building a portfolio. One central question about investment objectives and fund composition is considered essential to address before selecting an ETF for the first time.
Best Oil Stocks Right Now
The oil sector continues to attract investor attention amid a combination of international supply chain pressures and ongoing geopolitical events affecting the transport of natural resources. Energy markets have experienced significant volatility in recent years, driven by factors ranging from global health crises to regional conflicts that disrupt production and distribution. Several oil companies have emerged as notable performers within the sector given current market conditions.
Alan Greenspan, the legendary former Federal Reserve chair, dies
Alan Greenspan, who served as chair of the Federal Reserve from 1987 to 2006, has died. During his nearly two-decade tenure, Greenspan was widely regarded as one of the most effective central bankers in modern history, navigating the US economy through multiple crises and periods of expansion. His legacy was later complicated by criticism linking his deregulatory policy stance to conditions that contributed to the 2008 financial crisis, the worst since the Great Depression.
