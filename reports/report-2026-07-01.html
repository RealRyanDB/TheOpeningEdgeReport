CAPITAL IDEAS: What happens to the stock market if the Federal Reserve raises interest rates? - The Berkshire Edge
A rise in Federal Reserve interest rates historically places downward pressure on stock valuations, as higher borrowing costs reduce corporate profit margins and make bonds more attractive relative to equities. The Fed uses rate increases as a primary tool to combat inflation, a dynamic that has been central to market behavior in recent years. Elevated rate expectations have continued to weigh on equity market sentiment heading into the second half of 2026.
