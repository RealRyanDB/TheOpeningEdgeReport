News & Catalyst Briefing

Anthropic moves closer to mega-IPO as bankers line up investor meetings
Anthropic is organizing investor meetings in preparation for a potential IPO as early as October 2026. The AI company, known for its Claude models, is positioning itself to reach public markets ahead of rival OpenAI. A successful listing would mark one of the most significant technology IPOs in recent years given Anthropic's rapid growth and high valuation.
Stocks climb further on Fed, earnings optimism - BusinessWorld Online
Equity markets extended their gains as investors responded positively to signals from the Federal Reserve and an encouraging start to the corporate earnings season. Expectations that the Fed may hold or ease rates have supported risk appetite across major indices. Strong early earnings results have reinforced confidence in the broader economic outlook.
Google Ordered to Give A.I. Rivals More Access on Android Smartphones
European Union regulators have ordered Google to open up its Android platform to competing artificial intelligence services. The decision stems from concerns that Google could leverage its dominant Android user base to entrench its own AI products at the expense of rivals. The ruling represents a significant regulatory intervention in how AI services are distributed on mobile devices across the EU.
US tried permanent daylight saving time once before. It didn't last
The United States previously implemented permanent daylight saving time during a trial period from 1974 to 1975 under President Nixon, who signed the Emergency Daylight Saving Time Energy Conservation Act in response to the Arab oil embargo. The measure was intended to reduce fuel consumption during the energy crisis but proved unpopular with the public, particularly due to dark winter mornings. Congress ultimately reversed the policy before the trial period was completed.
US Jobless Claims Decline to 208,000, Below Forecasts
Initial applications for US unemployment benefits fell to 208,000 last week, coming in below analyst expectations. The decline indicates that layoffs remain relatively low and that the American labor market continues to hold steady. The data adds to a broader picture of labor market resilience despite ongoing uncertainty in the macroeconomic environment.
