Stocks Fall Ahead of Alphabet as Brent Hits $95: Markets Wrap
US equities declined ahead of Alphabet Inc.'s earnings report as investors looked for signals on whether hyperscaler AI spending is generating returns. Brent crude rising to $95 per barrel added further pressure on market sentiment. Alphabet's results are expected to be a key data point for the broader technology sector's near-term direction.
Goldman Sachs creates private markets platform as rich investors seek the next SpaceX and Stripe
Goldman Sachs is launching a new alternative investments platform designed to give wealthy clients and family offices direct access to stakes in private companies. Demand for private market exposure has grown as high-profile firms like SpaceX and Stripe have remained outside public markets for extended periods. The platform represents Goldman's effort to capture a larger share of the expanding private wealth management space.
Jamie Dimon says he wouldn't buy stocks or Treasurys at current prices
JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon stated he would not purchase stocks or long-term Treasury bonds at their current price levels. Dimon cited geopolitical instability and fiscal risks as factors he believes are not adequately reflected in current market valuations. His comments add to a growing chorus of senior financial executives expressing caution about asset prices.
Stock Market Outlook Hinges On AI Earnings And Fed Rate Risks - Forbes
The near-term direction of US equity markets is closely tied to incoming earnings results from major artificial intelligence-linked companies. Federal Reserve rate policy remains an additional variable, with uncertainty around the timing and pace of any future adjustments. The convergence of these two factors has left investors closely monitoring both corporate results and central bank communications.
Bank of America CEO warns inflation will back Fed into a corner
Bank of America CEO Brian Moynihan warned that persistent inflation could limit the Federal Reserve's ability to maneuver on interest rate policy. Elevated price pressures have already kept the Fed on a cautious path, reducing flexibility to cut rates even as economic conditions shift. The warning reflects broader concern among major bank executives that inflation dynamics could constrain monetary policy options in the months ahead.
