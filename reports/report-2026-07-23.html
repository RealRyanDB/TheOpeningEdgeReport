John Paulson says we are in the early stages of a long-term bull market for gold
Billionaire investor John Paulson has declared that gold is in the early stages of a sustained long-term bull market. Central banks have been steadily adding bullion to their reserves, and that institutional demand has been joined by growing interest from the private sector. The broadening base of buyers has reinforced Paulson's conviction that the rally in gold prices has significant room to run.
Google Hit With $1 Billion Fine By EU Over Search Engine Practices
The European Union has fined Google $1 billion over anti-competitive practices related to its search engine business. The penalty arrives at a particularly sensitive moment for trans-Atlantic trade relations, adding regulatory pressure on one of the world's largest technology companies. Google now faces the dual challenge of managing the financial penalty and addressing the EU's underlying competition concerns.
Cathie Wood says battered SpaceX could become 'most important company in global history'
ARK Invest CEO Cathie Wood remains firmly bullish on SpaceX, calling it a candidate to become the most important company in global history. ARK allocated $80 million to SpaceX following its IPO, even as the stock has shed nearly $1.4 trillion in market value from its peak. Wood's position reflects a long-term view on the company's potential despite the steep decline in its share price since listing.
Cellnex Studies Strategic Options After Share Price Slump
European wireless tower operator Cellnex Telecom is evaluating a range of strategic options as it contends with a heavy debt load and a prolonged weakness in its share price. The company has been working through the financial pressures that have weighed on its valuation in recent periods. No specific outcome has been announced, and the review of options remains ongoing.
StorageVault Grows YTD Revenue 10.3%, NOI 9.7% and AFFO 8.8% in Q2 2026, Completes Acquisitions, Adds Expanded Space, and Increases Dividend
StorageVault Canada reported year-to-date revenue growth of 10.3%, net operating income growth of 9.7%, and adjusted funds from operations growth of 8.8% through the second quarter of 2026. The Toronto-based self-storage company also completed new acquisitions and added expanded storage space during the period. StorageVault announced an increase to its dividend alongside the Q2 results.
